Topic 6: Metaphysics of Race

For this short unit, we look at a different question in metaphysics (the study of what there is, or what exists). Our last metaphysics question was What is personal identity? Now we ask Do races exist? We will spend this week on that.

For this reading, it is useful to know a philosophical term: “realist.” In Philosophy, someone who is a realist about X believes that X is real (for any X). So, for example, a realist about race is someone who believes that races exist. One important reason to clarify this is that the phrase “race realism” is often used by non-philosophers to describe the importantly different view that some races are superior to others (see Wikipedia).

Reading

  1. Michael Root. 2000. “How We Divide the World.” Philosophy of Science 67:S628–S639. 10 pages.
  2. Quayshawn Spencer. 2014. “A Radical Solution to the Race Problem.” Philosophy of Science 81 (5): 1025–1038.
