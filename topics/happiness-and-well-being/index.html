Topic 8: Happiness and Well Being

What should we aim at in life? To be happy, or something else?

Reading

  1. Epictetus, from Epictetus: The Discourses and Manual translated by P.E. Matheson, 1917. Available here
  2. Robert Nozick, “The Experience Machine” from Anarchy, State, and Utopia, 1974. Available through Blackboard.
  3. Valerie Tiberius, “Cultural Differences and Philosophical Accounts of Well-Being” in Journal of Happiness Studies; 5(3): 293–314, 2004. 20 pages. Available here.

See also

  • Epictetus on Wikipedia

  • Robert Nozick on Wikipedia

  • “How to Be a Stoic: An Evolving Guide to Practical Stoicism for the 21st Century,” a collection of videos, writings, interviews, and such by Massimo Pigliucci, author of How to Be a Stoic.

  • Admiral James Bond Stockdale “Courage Under Fire: Testing Epictetus's Doctrines in a Laboratory of Human Behavior” (1993), here, excerpt:

    On September 9, 1965, I flew at 500 knots right into a flak trap, at tree-top level, in a little A-4 airplane—the cockpit walls not even three feet apart—which I couldn’t steer after it was on fire, its control system shot out. After ejection I had about thirty seconds to make my last statement in freedom before I landed in the main street of a little village right ahead. And so help me, I whispered to myself: “Five years down there, at least. I’m leaving the world of technology and entering the world of Epictetus.”

  • Nancy Sherman, “A Crack in the Stoic’s Armor” (2010), a response to Stockdale by a moral philosopher, writing in The New York Times, here.

  • Stoicism in the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy by Dirk Baltzly (2018).
