Topic 3: Logic

A basic organizing structure for philosophy is the argument. Arguments are sets of statements where one of the statements is a conclusion. What knits them together is logic. We will think about what arguments are and then learn some basic ideas of logic.

Reading

  1. “Basic Ideas and Tools,” Chapter 1 of A Modern Formal Logic Primer by Edmund Teller, sections 1-1 to 1-4, or equivalently pages 1–10 of Volume 1. (PDF download available here, made available without charge by the author).
  2. “Square of Opposition” from The Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy

See also

  • The rest of Teller’s A Modern Formal Logic Primer is available for free! Feel free to keep reading.
  • “The Traditional Square of Opposition” in The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy
  • “Quantifiers and Quantification” in The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy
