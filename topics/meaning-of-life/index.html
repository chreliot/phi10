Topic 9: Meaning of Life

Are there any facts about what we should aim for in life? What would anchor such facts? Does anything make life meaningful? How could we know? And what would be the alternative? Nihilism—that nothing matters? Or would life without an anchored meaning be absurd and pointless?

Reading

  1. Susan Wolf, “The Meanings of Lives” (2015) from The Variety of Values: Essays on Morality, Meaning, and Love, available here.
  2. Thomas Nagel, “The Absurd” (1971) The Journal of Philosophy 68(20), available here through JSTOR.

See also

  • “Meaning of Life” in the SEP
  • “The Myth of Sisyphus” by Albert Camus (1942)
  • “Morality and the Meaning of Life: Some First Thoughts.” by Norman Dahl (1987) Canadian Journal of Philosophy 17:1–22.
