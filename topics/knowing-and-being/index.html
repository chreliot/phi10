Topic 5: Knowing and Being

Here we meet skepticism, the worry that we can’t have genuine knowledge, and then accounts of how we can have knowledge. This introduces us to Epistemology. We then see how epistemology connects with Metaphysics, in seeing how accounts of how we know are related to accounts of what we are. We’ll do this by focusing on two early modern philosophers and their critics.

Reading

  1. René Descartes, “First Meditation and Second Meditation” from Meditations on First Philosophy in Which Are Demonstrated the Existence of God and the Distinction Between the Human Soul and Body. ed. Jonathan Bennett1
  2. John Locke, link to come

See also

  • Peter Adamson accessibly discusses other historical skeptics, before Descartes, like Nagarjuna, Nicholas of Autrecourt, and Sextus Empiricus on the History of Philosophy without Gaps podcast.
  • Thinking about optical illusions (nice collection of examples) is a great way to appreciate the idea that we only believe illusions are illusory because our senses are mostly trustworthy. My favorites are probably motion-induced blindness illusions.
