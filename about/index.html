Using this site

This site is for the PHI 10 Introduction to Philosophy course (Fall 2019) at Hofstra University.

In addition to this site, we will also use a Blackboard site. Blackboard is available through the password-protected Hofstra Portal. Why use both? This site is easy to access and to publish to; the Blackboard site can present password-protected information. Your grades will be available through Blackboard. There is a link to Blackboard at the top of this (and every) page ↑.

Structure of this site

All the site’s posts are available on the home page in chronological order. There are also shortcut buttons at the top if you want to focus on just posts about topics or tests.

  • Topics pages describe topics, which normally span several class sessions. They list Required reading assignments, written assignments you’ll need to turn in, further, not-required content you may be interested in under See also, and Afternotes, if any. “Afternotes” refers to follow-up items from class, things students send me, and similar.
  • Tests pages gradually accumulate test questions for your first test (= “midterm exam”) and second test (= “final exam”). As there are two tests, there will be a list of possible questions for each one.

Share things

Think of anything that reminds you of what we’re talking about in the course? Students sometimes send me things others might enjoy seeing. It’s straightforward for me to add such things to the topics pages.
